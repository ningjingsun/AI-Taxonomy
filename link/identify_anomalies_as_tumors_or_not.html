Medical Imaging Analysis


This feature is a technique to detect anomalies in medial images of a body part during clinical analysis, and identify the types of the anomalies by comparing the features of the anomalies against features of different types of tumors. This feature could help doctors and nurses to identify potential diseases of patients faster and more efficiently. This feature could also help practitioners to avoid personal biases and improve the accuracy of diagonosis. This feature could help medical practitioners to create more accurate diagnosis and prognosis, thus increasing the revenues and efficiency of the hospitals and reducing labor costs of the industry.


Industry: Healthcare
Examples: Mammogram screenings/analysis, MRI brain analysis, fractures/musculoskeletal injuries, Cardiac imaging
Service Type: B2C

Sequences

1. Detect anomalies in medical images  >  2. Identify anomalies as tumors or not  >  3A. Identify tumors as either benign or malignant

Input Data:


Medical images with labels of anomalies; datasets of features of tumors

Output Data:


Label saying if the detected anomaly is a tumor or not

Data Features:


Image data set of healthlly features, image data set of abnormal features, 2D/3D scan of image in question
